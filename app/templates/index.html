a TAFLab-Research

I am currently an undergraduate research assistant in the Berkeley Theoretical and Applied Fluid Dynamics Laboratory. I am a member of a team working on the US Department of Energy Waves to Water Prize. This past semester, I created simulator in Matlab that computed time-domain results for a rotary pump connected to a floating wave energy absorber. I also wrote software to determine the optimal flywheel weight, and spool diameter to maximize the energy capture of our pumping system at various wave frequencies and amplitudes.

I am a member of the Vehicle Dynamics subteam of the Berkeley Formula SAE Team. In my year on the team, I have redesigned our compliance testing procedures, and been heavily involved in our suspension setup and tuning process.

a Battlebots

I competed in Battlebots 2020 with team ShellShock. Our robot was a circular chassis surrounded by a 150lb steel dome spinning 3000rpm. The robot also used electromagnets and retractable wheels to fix itself to the steel floor with over a 1000lbs of force to avoid getting flipped. I helped with the CAD design of the chassis and the retractable wheels, which were springloaded so that under normal load they lifted us just above the ground, while with the electromagnets on they fully retracted into the chassis.

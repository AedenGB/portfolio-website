a Employment a Wavemaker Labs - Mechanical Engineering Intern

During the Spring of 2020, I was a Mechanical Engineering Intern at Wavemaker Labs. I was mostly involved with Graze, an autonomous industrial lawn mower. I helped design the drive system kinematics, trying to minimize lateral motion of the blade deck, even when turning. I also worked on the path-planning algorithm for the mower, which, in addition to deciding on an optimal gras-cutting path, reacted to obstacles and re-routed itself to correct for them. To determine what slopes the mower was stable on, and to estimate loads on various mower components, I wrote a static model for the mower that calculated wheel loads based on the positions of the heaviest components inside the chassis.

a TAFLab - Research Apprentice

I am currently an undergraduate research apprentice in the Berkeley Theoretical and Applied Fluid Dynamics Laboratory. I am a member of a team working on the US Department of Energy Waves to Water Prize. My first contribution to the project was a simulator in Matlab that computed time-domain results for a rotary pump connected to a floating wave energy absorber. Using this simulator, I made an optimizer to determine the optimal flywheel weight, and spool diameter to maximize the energy capture of our pumping system at various wave frequencies and amplitudes. Most recently, I have been working on software to choose the exact parameters for a custom high-pressure rotary water pump that we are constructing. The sizes and positions of pistons on a crankshaft are optimized so that we can adapt the pump’s displacement easily to changing ocean conditions.

a Projects a Plastic Harmonic Drive Development

Since June 2020, a friand and I have been working on making the worlds best plastic harmonic drive. We have experimented primarily with 3D printed prototypes, made of ABS, PLA, CF-impregnated-Nylon, and various photopolymers printed with several different SLA Printers. Our current prototype is a zero-backlash gearbox weighing less than 100g, with output torque around 50 Nm. The tooth profiles are a new design, with a point-cloud computed specifically for maximum tooth contact on a harmonic drive.

a Formula Racing - Vehicle Dynamics Team Lead

I lead the Vehicle Dynamics subteam of the Berkeley Formula SAE Team. I am responsible for managing the VD subsystem, which designs the car’s suspension geometry, runs all testing and simulation of the car, and produces overall vehicle design specs, such as tire compound/size, final drive ratio, CP location, and much more. In my time on the team, I have redesigned our compliance testing procedures, added tire-temperature modeling to our in-house lap simulation software, and designed several new tools to analyze dynamic testing data. Recently, I designed an addition to team car-simulation software to evaluate an electronic brake-bias-by-speed system currently being considered for the car.

a Battlebots

I competed in Battlebots 2020 with team ShellShock. Our robot was a circular chassis surrounded by a 150lb steel dome spinning 3000rpm. The robot also used electromagnets and retractable wheels to fix itself to the steel floor with over a 1000lbs of force to avoid getting flipped. I helped with the CAD design of the chassis and the retractable wheels, which were springloaded so that under normal load they lifted us just above the ground, while with the electromagnets on they fully retracted into the chassis.
